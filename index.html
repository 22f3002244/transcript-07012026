The Industry Problem:

Post-COVID, we've witnessed an increasing trend in the live event industry. India's biggest ticket marketplace, BookMyShow, despite being aware of illegal activities, appears to support them for personal benefits. They now produce events and manage everything from A to Z. Even if someone files a complaint, they can show booking details, leaving no legal recourse.

Case Study: Coldplay Concerts (January 2025)
The event took place in India for 5 days: 3 days in Mumbai and 2 days in Gujarat.
Assuming the cricket stadium had a total capacity of 1 lakh people:
  • 5 days = 5 lakh people
  • Average selling price: ₹10,000
  • Total sales: 10,000 × 5,00,000 = ₹500 crore
This represents nearly ₹500 crore in business from a single event. The majority of tickets were resold on platforms like Viagogo and listed for lakhs of rupees.

Zomato's Entry:

Observing this trend, Zomato brought Dua Lipa, a singer from the UK, for their Zomato Feeding India event. However, it flopped as 80% of tickets were being sold outside the venue at 50% less than MRP. They capitalized on the craze, did an IPO, acquired Paytm Insider for ₹2,000 crore, and created District as a ticketing platform.

District's Strategy:
  • Provides minimum guarantee (MG) to organizers to list events on their platform instead of BookMyShow
  • Introduced QR ticketing, where tickets can be shared with others, enabling easy resale
  • Introduced a "pay 50% now, rest later" option to achieve sales targets more easily

Unfair Practices:

BookMyShow has acquired and managed different companies for promotion and sales:
  • Townscript: Platform with presence in Pune and Singapore—BookMyShow funded it and now controls event listings there
  • Spacebound: Allegedly took over India's most successful music festival, Sunburn, illegally and now owns the IP. A ₹300 crore case has been filed against Spacebound and BookMyShow by the owners of Sunburn (originally owned by Percept Limited)
BookMyShow uses these IPs and companies as co-hosts and for offline ticket sales. For example, Sunburn festivals are produced by Spacebound (acquired by BookMyShow), sold online on BookMyShow, and sold offline by Sunburn PRs.

Offline event sales involve getting a deal from the organizer, selling at online MRP, and earning 10-20% commission on sales.

My Startup Solution: SortMyEntries

I'm creating a website myself and recently finalized my user interface. I'm planning to create a structured system where I'm introducing a card, similar to an ATM card, which will be generated for each user account.

How the Card Works:
  • Acts as a ticket to every event
  • Contains a unique 14-digit key linked to the user account and tickets booked
  • Available digitally on the website/app (no need to carry physical card)
  • Scanned at entry gate for automatic ticket verification
  • Linked with government ID for age verification
  • Cannot be shared with others—eliminates resale opportunities
Verification of other ticket holders will be carried out manually at the entry gate. Any breach of rules will restrict entry for all tickets purchased under that booker ID.

Card Benefits:
  • Prepay to SortMyEntries and load credits on the card
  • Fast payment at booking time (no payment gateway redirects or OTP verification)
  • Credit limit system (e.g., standard card has ₹7,500 maximum limit)
  • Upgradeable for higher limits (valid for one year)
  • Free card upon first account creation
  • Replaceable if lost (digital version always available on web/app)
  • Unique ID is hashed and non-readable—prevents duplicate creation

Additional Features:

SortMyEntries also provides business tools for organizers, including insights, AI analysis, and an AI assistant.
